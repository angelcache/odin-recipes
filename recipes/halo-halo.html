Halo Halo

Home

Halo-halo (meaning "mix-mix" in Filipino) is a colorful and refreshing Filipino dessert made with a blend of crushed ice, sweetened fruits, jellies, beans, and other local delicacies, all layered in a tall glass. It's topped with leche flan, ube halaya (purple yam jam), and a scoop of ice cream, then drizzled with evaporated milk. Meant to be stirred together before eating, halo-halo offers a delightful mix of textures and flavors — cold, creamy, chewy, and sweet — perfect for beating the tropical heat.

Ingredients

  • Shaved Ice
  • Evaporated Milk
  • Plantains / Bananas
  • Ube
  • Beans
  • Coconut
  • Sugar palm fruit / jackfruit
  • Flan

Steps

  1. Place jack fruit in a bowl. Add white beans and red beans and mix thoroughly.
  2. Place 2 tablespoons of the jackfruit mixture in a tall glass. Fill glass 2/3-full of shaved ice. Pour evaporated milk over the shaved ice. Mix using a teaspoon. Top with 2 tablespoons vanilla ice cream. Repeat layering the halo halo in 3 more glasses.
